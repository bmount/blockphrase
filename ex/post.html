Last week I saw a new-to-me project from a few years ago, by French artist Armelle Caron (cartiste? artographer? We need a new word for things like this .) She had broken down a number of cities into the shapes created by their street geometries:

Above is Tamarac, Florida, a product of 60's US car culture (it spells "Car-a-Mat", the name of the original developer's chain of automatic carwashes, backwards.) I thought it would be interesting to make a sort of general block viewer for that kind of visual — just the shapes of places that are not streets. Here's Tamarac again:

Fun fact about that leftmost block: all 5 streets in its lower-right are called Malvern Drive. Just looking at a map it's hard to get a sense of the actual level of connectivity the streets enable. It's hard to tell that the effective distance across this sidewalk:

is 6 blocks. Here is another town, at the same scale and with a Tamarac-like name — Tacuarembó, Uruguay, one of my favorite places on Earth:

There are many interesting differences between suburban Florida and northern Uruguayan regional capitals — but I think that diagram captures a very important one. (It really is all like that.) Here's Rome:

To use, navigate to a place of interest in the labeled black and white finder map → here. The "create diagram" button will render a square area about 4-6 blocks across, the middle of the graph-like figure is approximately the center of the finder map view. Cyan is optional but recommended. Underlying map imagery comes from the indomitable lions of open source web mapping at Stamen Design. The code for this project is available here.
